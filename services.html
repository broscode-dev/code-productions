Structural concert production refers to the technical and logistics aspects of organizing and executing a live concert event infrastructure.

This involves a range of tasks and responsibilities, including:

Venue Selection: Choosing an appropriate location for the concert based on factors such as capacity, accessibility, acoustics, weather conditions and topography.

Structure Design & Construction Designing and building the temporary tent, stage and related infrastructure, such as raised platforms, truss works, masking, chairs, barricades, mats, etc.
Audio production in live events refers to the planning, implementation, and management of sound systems and equipment for live performances.

The process of audio production in live events involves:

Planning and preparation: Works closely with the event organizers and performers to understand their audio requirements.

Equipment selection and setup: Selects and sets up the appropriate audio equipment.

Soundcheck and testing: Conducts a sound check to test the audio equipment and ensure that the sound levels and quality are appropriate for the venue.

Monitoring and adjusting: Monitors the sound levels and adjusts the audio equipment as needed to maintain the desired sound quality.

Post-production and recording: Performs works such as mixing and mastering the audio recordings.
Backline production in live events refers to the equipment and instruments that are placed on stage for the performers to use during their performance. This can include items such as drums, guitar and bass amplifiers, keyboards, and other musical equipment.

The backline technician works closely with the artist's team to ensure that the specific equipment and instruments requested by the artist are available and properly set up for their performance.

Backline production is an important aspect of live events as it enables the performers to focus on their performance without having to worry about the technical aspects of the equipment.

Overall, backline production is a critical component of live events that require technical expertise and attention to detail to ensure that the performers have everything they need to deliver a successful performance.

LED wall production in live events refers to the use of LED video walls as a backdrop or visual element of the performance. They create visually stunning backdrops, display dynamic video content, and enhance the overall visual experience of the audience.

The process of LED wall production in live events involves:

Conceptualization and design: We develop a concept and design that complements the performance and venue.

Equipment selection and setup: Based on the LED wall design, the team selects and sets up the appropriate LED panels, video processors, and other equipment.

Content creation and programming: The content to be displayed will be handled by the team.

Monitoring and adjusting: During the event, the team monitors the LED wall display and adjusts it as needed.
Media production in live events involves the creation, integration, and presentation of various media elements such as videos, images, audio, and other digital content, into live event production.

Media production in live events involves:

Conceptualization and planning: Works closely with event organizers, performers, etc to gather requirements and then develop a concept and plan that outlines the media elements that will be integrated into the event.

Pre-production: Creates and develops the media content, such as videos, graphics, and other digital assets, that will be used in the live event.

Integration and presentation: Integrates and presents the media elements, such as video projections, sound effects, and lighting, to enhance the audience's experience and complement the performance.

Post-production: Includes editing and colour grading, to prepare the media content for distribution or future use.
Artist Management in live events refers to the process of handling the professional affairs and career development of performers, such as musicians, actors, and other artists.

The artist manager is responsible for artist's live events, including booking performances, negotiating contracts, arranging transportation and accomodation, coordinating with event promoters and organizers, and ensuring that the artist is prepared for their performance.

In addition to logistical responsibilities, artist management also involves promoting the artist's brand and building their fan base through marketing. This includes creating social media content, coordinating interviews with media outlets, and working with sponsors to secure endorsements and partnerships.

Overall, artist management in live events is an essential function that requires strong organizational and communication skills, as well as a deep understanding of the music and entertainment industry.
Social Media Management in live events refers to the process of managing and creating content for social media platforms to promote and engage with attendees during the event.

It involves developing a social media strategy that aligns with the event's goals and target audience. This includes identifying the relevant social media platforms to use, such as Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, and creating content that resonates with the audience.

The content can include pre-event promotion, live updates during the event, and post-event follow-up. Live updates can include photos, videos, and text-based posts that showcase the event's highlights and encourage attendees to share their own experiences.

Social media management also involves monitoring and engaging with attendees on social media, such as responding to comments, and messages and addressing any concerns or questions in a timely manner.
Event security in live events refers to the process of managing and ensuring the safety and security of attendees, staff and performers during an event. It is a critical component of live events as it helps to prevent incidents, minimise risks, and create a safe environment for all participants.

Event security typically involves a range of measures, including physical security, such as access control, bag checks, and metal detectors, as well as staffing security, such as security guards, ushers, and event coordinators.

Event security also involves developing emergency plans and protocols for different scenarios such as medical emergencies, natural disasters, or security threats. These plans should include a clear chain of command, communication protocols, and evacuation procedures to ensure the safety of all attendees and staff.

Event security also involves risk assessment and management, which requires identifying potential risks, such as overcrowding, potential protests, or security breaches, and taking proactive measures to prevent them.

Air conditioning production refers to the planning and implementation of cooling systems to regulate the temperature and humidity levels in event venues, such as concert halls, convention centres, and outdoor festivals.

The process of air conditioning production involves:

Assessment of the venue: Assess the size and layout of the event venue, as well as the expected number of attendees, to determine the appropriate cooling system requirements.

Selection of cooling systems: Selects the appropriate AC units, such as central AC systems, portable AC units, or evaporative coolers.

Installation and set-up: Includes determining the location and number of units needed, as well as any necessary electrical or plumbing connections.

Monitoring and maintenance: During the event, systems are monitored to ensure they are functioning properly and maintaining the desired temperature and humidity levels.
Special effects (FX) refers to the use of various technical elements, such as lighting, sound, and visual effects, to create a unique and immersive experience for the audience.

Some examples of special FX include:

Pyrotechnics: The use of fireworks, flames, and other explosive effects to create a spectacle.

Lighting effects: The use of specialized lighting equipment, such as lasers, strobe lights, and moving lights, to create a dynamic and visually stunning atmosphere.

Sound effects: The use of sound equipment and techniques, such as surround sound, reverberation, and sound effects, to create an immersive audio experience.

Visual effects: The use of video projection, LED screens, and other visual effects to create a dynamic and engaging visual display.
